about

From a fireteam to a build pipeline.

Before software, I was an 11B Infantryman in the 101st Airborne Division. Combat Infantry Badge, Purple Heart, Afghanistan, 2011 to 2014. That work ran on clear ownership, real consequences, and no half-measures. So do the systems I ship now.

Seven years shipping production backends across healthcare SaaS, legal tech, and enterprise platforms. At Carefeed I own the EHR integration architecture end to end: data exchange across nine-plus platforms (Epic, PointClickCare, MatrixCare), the webhook and PDF/OCR pipelines that hundreds of facilities depend on, and the auth layer behind a multi-tenant SaaS. When I inherited a four-year backlog of 27,000-plus failed uploads, I fixed the root cause and shipped the cleanup tooling that brought integrity back above 99 percent. This is the work: systems where a wrong record is a regulatory finding, not a failed unit test.

Alongside the day job, my side work concentrates on the infrastructure around an LLM, not the model itself: runtime governance for agents (Imara, on npm), MCP gateways (Mavryn), and capability-broker architecture for local agents in regulated industries (Castellan).

MBA from Temple. BA from Pomona. Since age 18 I've also owned and run a farm in Kitale, Kenya, the kind of standing P&L responsibility that makes most engineering tradeoffs feel routine.

FIG. 03 – Imara governance proxy · tamper-evident audit

Imara

Runtime governance proxy for MCP. Intercepts every tool call, enforces YAML policy (allow, deny, rate-limit, escalate), and writes a tamper-evident, SHA-256 hash-chained audit log: genesis-anchored, with truncation detection. Published on npm.

TypeScript · MCP · npm signed
FIG. 04 – Castellan capability broker · refusal first-class

Castellan

Capability-broker architecture for delegated authority in local AI agents, applied to industrial maintenance work-order triage. The agent never holds authority. It asks a small, inspectable broker, which grants the capability or treats refusal as a first-class outcome, and records every decision to a hash-chained audit substrate.

Architecture · Local LLMs · Spec-first signed
FIG. 05 – Muhuri delegation credential · splice-proof, offline

Muhuri

A single compact credential that carries an entire AI-agent delegation chain, human to tool, and proves offline who authorized whom to do exactly what. Each hop can only narrow scope; the links are cryptographically bound so the chain cannot be spliced, and a stolen credential is inert without a holder-of-key proof. Ed25519 and canonical CBOR; verified with no server round-trip.

Ed25519 · CBOR · Offline-verify signed

Castellan: delegated authority for local AI agents

Six specifications and thirteen architectural decision records for a proof-of-concept on delegated authority in local AI agent systems, applied to industrial maintenance work-order triage. Written from the auditor's perspective. The thesis: an industrial buyer shouldn't have to trust the AI. They should have to trust the authority broker and the audit substrate, which are small, inspectable, and behave like things they already know how to evaluate.
